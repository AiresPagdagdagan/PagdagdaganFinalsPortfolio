Reflection #4

R4 : Information Architecture

The focus of information architecture is on effective, sustainable organization, structuring and labeling of contents so that the main objective of this is to provide users to complete tasks. And, you must also understand how the pieces fit together and how the objects relate to each other inside the structure. Information Architecture aim is to help users understand where they are, what they found, and what they can expect. As a consequence, the Information Architecture informs the content strategy by defining terms and informing the design and interaction of the user interface through its role in cable framing and prototyping processes.

These are the main components of Information Architecture Organization Schemes and Structures. How you categorize and structure information Labeling Systems ,How you represent information Navigation Systems, How users browse or move through information Search Systems, How users look for information You need to consider the interdependent character of users, content and meaning to construct these information systems. This is called "info-ecology," it refers to the following business goals, funding, politics, culture, technology, and resources, constraints Content: content objectives, document and data types, volume, existing structure, governance and ownership Users: audience, tasks, needs, information seeking behavior, experience. The Roles of Information Architects and UX Designers the Information architecture of knowledge is the essence of a website's experience and forms the foundation of it. Information architects draw from diverse information sets useful content frameworks and navigation systems. Before adding features, you must build website architecture. Infoarchitects build site maps and develop navigation systems that can then be incorporated into UX designs. The result is an attractive website with high quality that effectively communicates its information architecture and facilitates interaction with its elements of navigation when information architects collaborate with UX designers.
